I
About Fire Notification

What it does

Fire Notification (FN) is a real-time incident feed. When a fire department pages an incident, FN pushes the data into HubSpot and creates a new LotiProperty with all the property, owner, valuation, and incident details already populated. Jack and the team get the lead the moment it happens, without manual entry.

This is the primary inbound source for Loti's LA Fires pipeline. Most incidents Loti works originate here.

Why it matters

  • Speed. Properties land in HubSpot within seconds of being paged, ahead of competitors who rely on slower public data.
  • Coverage. Includes title, valuation, sale history, loan, and contact info, not just the address.
  • Reactive updates. If the incident escalates (alarm level upgraded, type reclassified, closed), HubSpot is updated automatically.

Note

V
FN Integration Workflow

Purpose

The FN Integration WFK workflow runs after FN writes to the "- FN" suffixed fields. It does two things:

  1. Format and clean the raw FN values using HubSpot Custom Code (whitespace, casing, type coercion, formatting).
  2. Copy each cleaned value into the original LotiProperty field used by the team in views, lists, and reports.

Until this workflow runs, the team only sees the raw "- FN" fields. The cleanup ensures the canonical LotiProperty fields stay consistent regardless of source.

Heads up

Because of the 1 minute Delay, expect a brief lag between the FN incident landing and the canonical fields being populated. Views that filter on canonical fields (not "- FN" fields) will show the property after the delay.

VI
Status & Limitations

Current status

Live. Incidents land in HubSpot in real time. The FN Integration WFK workflow runs on every new LotiProperty that matches either trigger group.

Known limitations

  • 4 events disabled on the FN side (out of 11 total). Not used by Loti today.
  • 4 fields not mapped (36 of 40 enabled). Anything FN sends outside the 36 mapped fields is dropped on the HubSpot side.
  • 1 minute delay before canonical fields populate. Avoid building automations that read canonical fields immediately on creation.
  • One way sync. Edits made in HubSpot are not pushed back to Fire Notification.
  • "- FN" duplication. Every mapped field exists twice on LotiProperty (raw FN copy + canonical). Keep this in mind when designing reports or list filters.
