About Loti

Loti Services, Inc. (loti.com) is an InsurTech company that helps homeowners recover after disasters. As licensed public adjusters, Loti represents the homeowner (not the insurance company) and manages the entire claim process. Founded after the 2019 Kincaid Fire, combining technology (3D LiDAR, AI Vision, AI Doc Scan) with expert public adjusting.

Licensed in California Oregon Washington Nevada Arizona and expanding.

Scoring Engine
TL

Two Scores, One Priority

▼
  • Engagement Score (contact-level) — what actions has this person taken? QR scan +20, page view +15, email click +5
  • FIT Score (property-level) — how strong is the opportunity? LA Fires = 100 fixed, BAU = 0-100 AI-generated
  • Opportunity Score = Engagement + FIT (0-200). Drives pipeline auto-movement and deal creation

Engagement Score — Complete Breakdown

Engagement Score lives at the contact level. It tracks real behavioral signals — not just opens. Points accumulate as the contact interacts with Loti content across all channels. The score resets to 0 after 30 consecutive days of no activity, ensuring only recently-engaged contacts score high.

FIT Score — How AI Evaluates BAU Properties

The FIT Score is calculated at the LotiProperty level by an AI model when a new incident arrives via Fire Notification. LA Fires properties skip AI scoring and receive a fixed score of 100 — every LA Fires property is considered maximum opportunity.

High-Weight AI Inputs
  • Incident severity (firefighter comments, structure involved)
  • Estimated property value (AVM Value)
  • Assessed total value (Assessed Total Value)
  • Primary dwelling coverage limit (policy data)
Medium-Weight AI Inputs
  • Ownership type (Property Ownership) — owner-occupied scores higher
  • Fire zone / CAL FIRE zone classification
  • Last sale price and purchase loan amount
  • Property class code
Lower-Weight / Contextual
  • Number of bedrooms / bathrooms
  • Year built (older = higher risk profile)
  • Vacant or abandoned flag
  • Type of home (SFR vs multi-family)
Score Outputs
  • LA Fires: Always 100
  • BAU 80–100: Strong opportunity. Likely to be sent to Jack for review.
  • BAU 40–79: Moderate. MQL email sequence.
  • BAU 0–39: Low. Passive monitoring only.
How Jack Score Fits In

Jack Score (0–100) is set manually by Jack after a field visit. It represents his ground-truth assessment of the opportunity — distinct from the AI-generated FIT Score. Jack's Score is recorded as Jack Score on the LotiProperty record and is visible in the Operational Dashboard.

Jack Score: High (70–100)

Strong in-person confirmation. Loss is significant, homeowner is receptive, insurance is accessible, no major blockers. Advance pipeline immediately.

Jack Score: Medium (30–69)

Some potential but uncertainty. May need follow-up visit. Insurance unclear, homeowner lukewarm, or damage lighter than FN data suggested.

Jack Score: Low (0–29)

Not viable. Loss too small, wrong property type, homeowner hostile, already represented, or insurance not eligible. Move to Closed Lost.

Not Yet Scored

Jack hasn't visited. Property sits in "Jack to Review" stage. Prioritised by FIT Score and drive distance until visited.

📈
Score hierarchy in practice: FIT Score surfaces the property → Engagement Score proves the homeowner is interested → Jack Score confirms the field reality. All three must align for a deal to close.

Stage-by-Stage Reference

All 8 stages in the Sales Pipeline, with win probabilities, entry criteria, and key actions required at each stage.

Stage 1 — Jack to Review 20% probability

How deals enter: Auto-created when LotiProperty hits the SQL minimum score, or manually created by Gerardo / Sol after enrichment. LA Fires deals auto-created on first engagement.

Jack's review criteria: Priority flag · FN Score · Property value (AVM) · Firefighter comments · Drive distance · Confirmed loss status
Outcome: Schedule visit (advance to Stage 2) OR disqualify (Closed Lost)
Stage 2 — Initial Contact Made no fixed probability

Entry criteria: Jack has made first contact — by phone, in person at property, or homeowner responded to mailer.

Key actions: Log call via RingCentral · Set Next Action Date · Note homeowner receptiveness · Schedule follow-up
Outcome: Homeowner is open to conversation (advance) OR not interested (Closed Lost with reason)
Stage 3 — Client Engaging 40% probability

Entry criteria: Active back-and-forth conversation. Homeowner is engaged and asking questions.

Key actions: Review insurance policy · Map damage categories (dwelling, personal property, LOU, secondary structures) · Assess claim potential · Set estimated dwelling claim and related fields · Begin working through objections
Stage 4 — Loti Qualified no fixed probability

Entry criteria: All 3 qualify conditions confirmed: (1) verifiable loss, (2) licensed state, (3) eligible insurance carrier and policy.

Key fields set: Loti Qualified = Yes · Insurance Carrier · Policy Number · Coverage Limits · Deductible · Primary Dwelling Limit
Key actions: Confirm insurance is accessible · Get adjuster details · Verify claim hasn't been closed
Stage 5 — Terms Proposed no fixed probability

Entry criteria: Representation agreement has been presented to homeowner with Loti's fee structure.

Key fields set: Fee Type (contingency/flat/hourly) · Fee Percentage · Loti Total Fee · Terms Agreed
Key actions: Follow up within 24–48h · Answer questions about fee structure · Set follow-up date
Stage 6 — Contract Signed — Won 100% probability

Entry criteria: Homeowner has signed the representation agreement.

Key fields set: Contract Sign date · Closed Won Reason · Amount (deal value)
Next: Active claim management begins. Contact lifecycle = Customer.
Stage 7 — Closed Lost 0% probability

Entry criteria: Deal will not close — homeowner declined, disqualified, or unresponsive.

Required fields: Closed Lost Reason · Disqualified Reasons
Re-nurture path: If Re-nurture Eligible = Yes, contact re-added to BAU or LA Fires follow-up email sequence

Internal name: 3348333282

What Counts as "Activity"

The Deal Stalled workflow monitors the following logged activities. If none of these occur within the 3-day window, the escalation chain fires.

Activities That Reset the Clock
  • Logged call (RingCentral or manual)
  • Email sent or received (tracked)
  • Note added to deal record
  • Task completed on deal
  • Meeting logged
  • Deal stage changed
Activities That Do NOT Reset the Clock
  • Call made from personal mobile (not logged)
  • Text sent outside HubSpot
  • Verbal conversation without a follow-up note
  • WhatsApp or personal email (not integrated)
  • Viewing the deal record without adding content
Escalation Chain Detail
Step 1 — Email Alert: Mike & Sean

Automatic email to Mike Yurochko and Sean Noonan. Includes: Deal name, deal stage, days since last activity, associated contacts, deal value, direct link to HubSpot record.

Step 2 — Slack Alert: Mike

Slack notification to Mike's workspace with a direct URL to the stalled deal in HubSpot. Mike can review on mobile within seconds.

Task created: "[Follow-Up Required] Why is this deal stalled?"

1. What is the current status of this deal?
2. When was the last time you spoke with the homeowner?
3. What is the next step and when will you complete it?
4. Is there a blocker preventing progress?
5. Should this deal be moved to a different stage or closed lost?

Step 4 — QA Review Task: Leadership

After Jack responds, a QA task is generated for Sean or Mike to verify the follow-up occurred and the deal has been moved appropriately. Closes the loop on the escalation.

Monitoring Coverage — All Active Deals

The monitoring workflow runs on all active deals (stages 1–5 of the pipeline). Closed Won and Closed Lost deals are excluded — they have no path to escalation. The "Deals To Be Deleted" stage is also excluded from monitoring.

Post-Creation Automation Chain

Three workflows fire automatically within seconds of a new LotiProperty being created by FN:

TL

LEAD SCORING

Engagement Score (behavior) + FIT Score (opportunity) = Opportunity Score (0-200)

Branch: INELIGIBLE

Score too low or doesn't meet criteria. Lifecycle = "Ineligible", suitability = "Not Suitable." No pipeline entry.

Branch: MQL

Moderate score. Added to Lead Nurturing Workflow:

BAU: Stage 1 → 2 → 3 (12+ emails each). Tracks: PA Warm / PA Cold / Suite.
LA Fires: Stage 1 → 2 (faster send schedule).
Goal: Build trust, bump score to SQL.
Branch: REMOTE

BAU California outside driving distance. Follows MQL email sequence but flagged as remote — no field visits.

Branch: SQL
1. Slack notification to Jack
2. Task: "Review and Attempt Contact" (next day, limited to 1/week)
3. If score >65: additional 3-day deadline task
Next: Jack reviews in AirCover briefing → field process begins

Nurture Track Detail — BAU vs LA Fires
BAU Nurture (3 Stages)
  • Stage 1 (PA Warm): 12+ emails. Educational, trust-building. Low urgency.
  • Stage 2 (PA Cold): Re-engagement. Different angle, higher urgency.
  • Stage 3 (Suite): Final attempt. Audience profiling by 4 types (pending).
  • Contact moves forward only if score increases. No increase = stays in stage or escalates to No Contact Flow.
LA Fires Nurture (2 Stages)
  • Stage 1: Immediate. Faster send schedule — shorter wait times, higher urgency messaging.
  • Stage 2: Re-engagement if Stage 1 doesn't convert.
  • LA Fires FIT Score is always 100 — so engagement score is the key factor for reaching SQL status.
  • LinkedIn outreach runs in parallel to follow-up email sequence.
⚠
Score Calculation Workflow: The Score Calculation workflow runs on the LotiProperty object and sums all linked contact Engagement Scores into a single property (Score). This combined view powers Jack's Operational Dashboard ranking. If a property has 3 owners and all 3 have engaged, scores stack — making multi-owner properties appear higher.

TL

Pipeline Walkthrough

New deal enters. Jack reviews LotiProperty data — AirCover intel, scores, photos, address.

Reached homeowner by phone, email, or in person. Is the homeowner receptive?

Exit: All attempts fail / client declines → Disqualified Reasons → Closed Lost. If re-nurture eligible → back to email nurture.
③ Client Engaging

Active conversation. Policy review, damage assessment, workstream mapping begins.

Insurance Info Collection

Carrier, policy number, coverage limits, deductible, endorsements, code/ordinance coverage.

LOTI QUALIFIED — Decision Point
YES: Loss confirmed + licensed state (CA/OR/WA/NV/AZ) + eligible insurance → advance to Loti Qualified stage.
NO: One condition failed → Disqualified Reasons → Closed Lost or re-nurture.
④ Loti Qualified → ⑤ Terms Proposed

Representation agreement prepared and presented. Fee structure and coverage terms shared.
