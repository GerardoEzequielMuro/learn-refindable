About Loti

Loti Services, Inc. (loti.com) is an InsurTech company that helps homeowners recover after disasters — fires, floods, storms, theft, and more. As licensed public adjusters, Loti represents the homeowner (not the insurance company) and manages the entire insurance claim process, from documentation to negotiation to settlement.

The company was founded after the Kincaid Fire of 2019 in Sonoma County, when founder Mike Yurochko lost much of his home and the recovery process took years. Loti was born to change that experience — combining technology (3D LiDAR scanning, AI Vision, AI Doc Scan) with expert public adjusting to deliver a six-star, hassle-free recovery for homeowners.

Loti is currently licensed in California Oregon Washington Nevada Arizona and actively expanding to more states.

💡 What is HubSpot?

A primer for team members who are new to the platform

HubSpot is the CRM (Customer Relationship Management) platform that Loti uses to manage every aspect of its sales, marketing, and customer operations. Think of it as the central brain of the business — it stores every contact, every property, every deal, every email, and every interaction in one place.

Key Concepts

Before diving into Loti's specific configuration, here are the fundamental HubSpot concepts you'll encounter throughout this documentation:

Contacts

Every person in the system — homeowners, referrals, leads. Each contact has properties (name, email, phone, etc.) and a history of every interaction they've had with Loti.

Deals

A potential revenue opportunity — in Loti's case, a homeowner who might sign a representation agreement. Deals move through stages in a pipeline (Jack to Review → Contract Signed).

Properties

Data fields attached to objects. For example, a contact has a "phone" property, a deal has an "amount" property. Loti has created many custom properties specific to the insurance adjusting business.

Workflows

Automated actions that happen when certain conditions are met. For example: "When a deal has no activity for 3 days, send an alert to Mike." Workflows are the autopilot of the CRM.

Pipelines

Visual representations of where deals stand in the sales process. Each column is a "stage" — deals move from left to right as they progress toward closing.

Lifecycle Stages

The journey a contact takes: Subscriber → Lead → MQL (Marketing Qualified) → SQL (Sales Qualified) → Opportunity → Customer. This helps segment who gets what communications.

How Loti Uses HubSpot

Loti's HubSpot instance (Account #243877040) is configured as a property-centric CRM — meaning the property (the house affected by a fire/disaster) is the central object, not the contact. This is a deliberate architectural decision that reflects the nature of the business: a single property might have multiple associated contacts (owner, spouse, family members, insurance agent), and the property is what determines the opportunity's value.

The custom object "LotiProperties" (internally called "Houses") is the hub that connects everything: contacts, deals, scoring, workflows, and pipeline stages all relate back to the affected property.

🏠
Key insight: When you're looking at a deal in HubSpot, always check the associated LotiProperty — that's where the core data lives (address, photos, scores, incident details). The deal is just the sales wrapper around the property opportunity.

📦 Products & Services

What Loti sells and how it connects to the CRM

Understanding what Loti offers is essential to understanding the CRM — every pipeline stage, scoring model, and email campaign is designed around these products:

Learning Hub (Free — Top of Funnel)

The Learning Hub is the largest free recovery library in the insurance industry. It includes 800+ articles called RallyBacks organized by incident type and recovery stage, thousands of insurance terms in plain language (Glossaries), and downloadable documents called Loti Docs (checklists, trackers, schedules). This is Loti's primary content engine and the main entry point for organic leads — homeowners searching for help after a disaster find the Learning Hub, consume content, and eventually connect with Loti's team.

📧
In HubSpot, the RallyBack Briefs newsletter (sent 2x/week) is the marketing vehicle for Learning Hub content. Newsletter signups come through a popup on the Learning Hub and Home Page.
RallyPro Services (Paid — Core Revenue)

This is Loti's core business: licensed public adjusting services. When a homeowner signs with Loti, the process follows five steps: (1) Personalized Consultation — 30-minute call to understand the situation, (2) Structured Workstreams — the claim is segmented into focused paths (Dwelling, Personal Property, ALE, Business Impacts), (3) Technology-Powered Documentation — 3D LiDAR scans, AI Vision for inventory, AI Doc Scan for receipts, Smart Ledger for payments, (4) Licensed Expert Negotiation — the adjuster coordinates with the insurer, and (5) Ongoing Support / Life 2.0 — annual policy health checks and seasonal RallyBacks.

Revenue model: Contingency fee — a percentage of the insurance settlement (standard for public adjusters). The deal properties fee_type, fee_percentage, and loti_total_fee track this in HubSpot.

The Suite (Tech Platform — Consumer Beta 2026)

Loti's technology stack (3D LiDAR, AI Vision, AI Doc Scan, Smart Ledger) is currently used internally by adjusters. A consumer-facing version is planned for 2026 with a SaaS subscription model. In HubSpot, the nurture track Suite exists to eventually segment contacts interested in this product.

🏠 LotiProperties (Custom Object)

The central hub of Loti's CRM architecture
🏠
LotiProperties is the most important object in the entire CRM. Internally called "Houses" in HubSpot, this custom object represents a physical property affected by a fire or disaster. Every contact, deal, score, and workflow connects through LotiProperties. When a new fire incident is detected by Fire Notification, a LotiProperty record is the first thing created.

Each LotiProperty record contains everything needed to evaluate an opportunity: the address and photos of the property, the AI-generated lead score, the property value estimate, the incident details from firefighters, and all associated contacts (owners, family members, neighbors). Jack uses this data every day to plan his field visits and prioritize which properties to visit first.

⚡ How Workflows Work

Understanding the automation layer

Workflows are HubSpot's automation engine — they watch for specific events (called triggers) and then execute a series of actions automatically. Think of them as "if this happens, then do that" rules running 24/7 in the background.

Loti has 15+ active workflows handling everything from lead scoring to deal creation to contact cleanup. They fall into three categories:

Deal Monitoring

Watches for stalled deals and alerts the team when opportunities are at risk of going cold. This is the most critical workflow in the system.

Scoring & Task Creation

Aggregates engagement scores, moves properties through the Opportunity Pipeline, creates follow-up tasks for Jack, and auto-creates deals when scores reach thresholds.

Data Management

Detects duplicate contacts, builds FN links, copies association labels between objects, handles contact cleanup, and syncs data between Houses and Contacts.

Email Marketing

Manages the BAU and LA Fires email nurture campaigns, no-contact follow-ups, and the welcome email for newsletter subscribers.

💡
Custom Code Workflows: Several of Loti's workflows use custom code actions (JavaScript) rather than standard HubSpot workflow steps. These include Duplicate Phone Detection, Association Label Copy, and Score Aggregation — they were built by Refindable to handle logic that's too complex for the standard workflow builder.

⚠️ Deal Monitoring

How the system catches stalled deals

The Deal Stalled Automation is arguably the most important workflow in Loti's HubSpot. In a business where speed-to-lead is critical (homeowners in crisis need help now), letting a deal go cold for even a few days can mean losing the opportunity to a competitor or simply losing the homeowner's trust.

⚠️ Deal Stalled Automation
LIVE
This workflow monitors every deal in the pipeline and fires when it detects inactivity. "Activity" means any logged call, email, note, meeting, or task. If none of these happen for 3+ consecutive days, the system escalates.
TriggerDeal has no logged activity for 3+ days (no calls, emails, notes, meetings, or tasks)
Action 1📧 Email notification → Sent to Mike Yurochko and Sean Noonan with deal details
Action 2💬 Slack alert → Sent to Mike with a direct link to the deal
Action 3📋 Task created for Jack: "[Follow-Up Required] Why is this deal stalled?" — includes 5 follow-up questions Jack must answer in the task notes before closing it
Action 4✅ QA Review Task: "[QA] Review Jack's Follow-Up Task Completion" — separate task for leadership to verify Jack filled in his notes properly
🚨
Known Issue: This workflow depends on call logging through RingCentral. If Jack makes calls but RingCentral isn't connected to HubSpot, those calls aren't logged — meaning the workflow sees "no activity" when there actually was activity. This creates false positive alerts that waste everyone's time. Completing the RingCentral integration is critical for this workflow to function correctly.

📧 Email Marketing Strategy

How Loti nurtures leads through email

Email is a critical part of Loti's lead nurture strategy. Not every homeowner is ready to sign with a public adjuster immediately — many are in shock, overwhelmed with decisions, or simply not aware of what a PA can do for them. The email system is designed to stay top-of-mind, build trust through educational content, and re-engage contacts when they're ready to take action.

The email ecosystem has several components working together:

Automated Nurture Campaigns

Multi-stage email sequences that drip content over weeks. BAU has 3 stages (36+ emails total), LA Fires has 2 stages (24+ emails). These run on autopilot once a contact is enrolled.

1:1 Sales Sequences

Personal emails from Zack's individual inbox (lotiadjusters.com domain). Text-only, human-feeling emails for high-priority contacts. Not automated campaigns — manually enrolled.

RallyBack Briefs (Newsletter)

Educational content newsletter sent 2x/week to all subscribers. Topics from the Learning Hub — fire safety, fall prevention, electrical safety, etc. Builds brand authority.

No Contact Flow

4-email sequence specifically for contacts who haven't responded to any outreach. Last-effort engagement before marking as unresponsive.

Domain Strategy

Loti uses two email domains for different purposes:

@loti.com ([email protected]) — Used for marketing emails, newsletters, and automated campaigns. This is the brand domain that recipients associate with Loti's content.
@lotiadjusters.com (mike@, jack@) — Used for 1:1 personal outreach and sales sequences. Text-only formatting makes emails feel like they came from a real person, not a marketing platform. Both mike@ and jack@ addresses are connected to HubSpot with active warm-up (running since January 20).

📝
Email warm-up is critical. New domains need 15-30 days of warm-up before sending at volume. The lotiadjusters.com addresses have been warming up since January and should be maintained as active even while running campaigns.

🚀 Lead Journey — Entry & Enrichment

How new leads enter the system and get processed

The lead journey begins when a new incident is detected or a homeowner finds Loti through any channel. Here's what happens step by step:

🔥 New Fire Notification Arrives

A residential fire is detected by the Fire Notification platform. The custom API integration automatically creates a LotiProperty record in HubSpot with incident details — address, severity, firefighter comments, ownership data. This is the starting point for the vast majority of leads.

Other entry points: Not all leads come from FN. Homeowners also find Loti through the website (form submissions), paid ads (Meta/Google), the Learning Hub newsletter signup, LinkedIn outreach (550 LA Fires contacts with automated connection requests), Postalytics direct mail (1,700+ mailers sent), BeenVerified research (manual imports by Sales Assistant), referrals from contractors/attorneys, and direct phone calls to 888-488-LOTI.
📊 Data Enrichment Process

Once a LotiProperty exists, the system automatically enriches it with additional data and runs three workflows:

Workflow 1 — Duplicate Phone Detection: Looks at all contacts associated with the property, normalizes their phone numbers (removing formatting differences), compares them against each other, and automatically adds a "Duplicate" label to any matches. All existing labels (Fire Notification, BeenVerified, Property Reach) are preserved.

Workflow 2 — FN Link Builder: Takes the Incident ID from the property record, appends it to the Fire Notification base URL, and writes the resulting clickable link back to the fn_link property. This gives the team one-click access to the original incident report.

Workflow 3 — Score Aggregation: Monitors all contacts associated with the property and sums their individual engagement scores. The total is written to the LotiProperty's engagement_score field, giving a property-level view of overall engagement.

🎯 Lead Journey — Scoring & Branches

How the system decides what happens to each lead
🧠 LEADSCORING

The scoring engine evaluates every lead on two dimensions: Engagement Score (how active is this person?) and FIT Score (how valuable is this property?). These combine into the Opportunity Score (0–200) which determines the next step.

Branch: INELIGIBLE

Score is too low, or the property doesn't meet basic criteria (wrong state, no verified loss, unsupported property type). The contact's lifecycle stage is set to "Ineligible" and suitability_status to "Not Suitable." They do not enter any pipeline. Journey ends here unless circumstances change.

Branch: MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)

Moderate score — the lead has potential but isn't ready for direct outreach. The contact is enrolled in the Lead Nurturing Workflow, which places them in the appropriate email campaign:

BAU contacts: Stage 1 → Stage 2 → Stage 3 (12+ emails each, dripped over weeks). Tracks: PA Warm / PA Cold / Suite.
LA Fires contacts: Stage 1 → Stage 2 (12+ emails). More aggressive cadence due to event urgency.
Goal: Build trust through content, re-engage the contact, and eventually bump their score high enough to move them to SQL.
Branch: REMOTE

BAU California contacts that are outside Jack's driving distance. They follow the MQL nurture path but are flagged as remote — outreach is phone/email only, no field visits. Campaign tag: BAU California (outside driving distance).

Branch: SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)

High score — this lead is ready for direct outreach. The system takes immediate action:

Step 1: Slack notification sent to Jack about the new SQL lead.
Step 2: Task auto-created: "Review and Attempt Contact – High Score Property (>65)." Due: next day. Max 1 task per contact per week (throttled to prevent overload).
Step 3: If score exceeds 65, an additional task is created with a 3-day deadline for review.
What happens next: Jack reviews the property in the AirCover briefing, decides whether to visit, and the field process begins.

🏗️ Lead Journey — Jack's Field Process

The daily routine from morning planning to end-of-day scoring

Jack Hall is the primary field representative. His daily routine is documented in the Jack's HubSpot Daily Workbook — a step-by-step guide that ensures consistent, high-quality field work with everything logged in HubSpot. Here's the complete process:

☀️ Morning Routine (Before Leaving)
Step 1 — Sales Workspace: Open HubSpot → Sales → Summary tab. This shows: total open tasks, high-priority count, pending to-dos, and outreach counters (calls/emails/LinkedIn — should be 0 if caught up).

Step 2 — Task Review: Click Tasks tab → filter by "Due Today" then "Overdue." Sort by High Priority first. Clear overdue tasks before starting new ones.
Task types Jack sees: "Review and Attempt Contact – High Score Property (>65)" · "[Follow-Up Required] Why is this deal stalled?" (answer 5 questions) · "[QA] Review Jack's Follow-Up Task Completion"

Step 3 — Route Planning: Open Loti Properties → sort by Priority (High first) then Distance to Rep → cluster nearby properties to minimize driving. Key columns: Priority, FN Score, Distance, Incident Date, House Owner, Loss Address, Possible Payout.
🏠 Before Each Visit — AirCover Briefing

Before knocking on any door, Jack opens the LotiProperty record and reviews all available intelligence:

Priority level · FN Score OpenAI Calibrated · Incident Date · Property Ownership (legal owner names) · Loss Address + City + Zip · Google Maps Link (copy to phone for navigation) · House Photo (confirm right property) · Owner Photo (recognize the homeowner) · Owner Occupied (Y/N) · All associated Contacts in the right panel with phone numbers and emails.
🔍 During the Visit — Field Assessment

Jack assesses the property in person and updates HubSpot immediately:

Damage assessment: Extent (Home Other structures Belongings) and Type (Total loss Partial loss Smoker).
Properties updated: confirmed_loss (Yes/No/Unknown) + loti_pa_fit (Not a Fit / Unknown Fit).
If homeowner shows interest: Update the deal stage on the associated Deal record.
🌙 End of Day — Close the Loop
Notes: Go back through every property visited → Activities tab → add detailed Note (condition of property, owner contact outcome, insurance situation, red flags, what was discussed). These notes are read by Mike, Sean, and the PA team.

Scoring: Fill in jack_score (0-100, honest assessment based on ground reality) + jack_score_reason (written justification — key details from what was observed).

Date: Set site_visit_date to today.

Tasks: Create follow-up tasks for properties where no one was home (title: "Review and Attempt Contact - [Incident ID]", due: next business day, High priority). Mark all completed tasks as done in Sales Workspace.
⚖️ Jack's Qualification Decision
IF DISQUALIFIED: Sets one of 19 Disqualified Reasons (No verified loss · Below threshold · Too old · Not in area · Insurance ineligible · Already represented · Chose competitor · Unresponsive · Not interested · Not Licensed · Felonies · Erratic behavior, etc.). If renurture_eligible = Yes → contact goes back to Lead Nurturing Workflow for another chance. Otherwise → deal moves to Closed Lost.

IF QUALIFIED: Two conditions trigger the next steps: either the loss is confirmed (confirmed_loss = Yes) or the property value exceeds $800K. This can trigger the Mailer Program (Postcard / $3 Offer / $10 Offer / Suite Mailer). A Deal is created automatically in the Sales Pipeline, and the deal progresses through the stages described in the next section.

🔄 Lead Journey — Deal Pipeline

From deal creation to contract or close
💡
Both BAU and LA Fires campaigns merge into the same Sales Pipeline. The loti__campaign property identifies which campaign each deal belongs to, but they all follow the same stages. The Deal Stalled Automation monitors every active deal regardless of campaign.
① Jack to Review

New deal enters the pipeline with properties auto-set: campaign tag, lead source, priority level. Jack reviews the associated LotiProperty data — AirCover intel, scores, photos, address — and decides on next steps.

② Initial Contact Made

Jack has successfully reached the homeowner by phone, email, or in person. The key question at this stage: is the homeowner receptive to learning about Loti's services?

Exit path: If all contact attempts fail or the client explicitly declines → set Disqualified Reasons → move to Closed Lost. If renurture_eligible = Yes, the contact re-enters the Lead Nurturing Workflow.
③ Client Engaging

Active, ongoing conversation. The homeowner is interested and sharing information. Jack begins reviewing their insurance policy, assessing the full scope of damage, and mapping out potential workstreams (Dwelling, Personal Property, ALE, Business Impacts). follow_up_move_foward is set to Yes/No based on whether the team should continue pursuing.

Insurance Info Collection

A critical data-gathering phase. The team collects: insurance carrier name, policy number and period dates, coverage limits for dwelling/personal property/ALE/secondary structures, deductible amount, endorsement details, and code/ordinance coverage. All tracked in dedicated deal properties.

⚖️ LOTI QUALIFIED — Decision Point
IF YES: Three conditions confirmed — (1) loss is real and covered, (2) property is in a licensed state (CA/OR/WA/NV/AZ), (3) insurance is eligible for PA representation. Properties set: loti_qualified = Yes, confirmed_loss = Yes, suitability_status = Suitable. Deal advances to stage ④.

IF NO: One or more conditions failed. Disqualified Reasons are set. If re-nurture eligible → Lead Nurturing Workflow. Otherwise → Closed Lost.
④ Loti Qualified

Fully vetted opportunity. Licensed state confirmed, insurance reviewed, loss documented. The team prepares the representation agreement with fee structure and coverage terms.

⑤ Terms Proposed

The representation agreement is presented to the homeowner. fee_type and fee_percentage are set. terms_agreed is set to Yes/No based on the homeowner's response. If terms are rejected, the deal may loop back or move to Closed Lost.

✅ Lead Journey — Close & Post-Claim

Contract signing and what comes after
✅ CONTRACT SIGNED — WON

contract_sign = Yes. The homeowner signs the representation agreement. Loti is now their licensed public adjuster and will manage the entire insurance claim process on their behalf.

Active Claim Management: The claim is segmented into workstreams — Dwelling, Personal Property, ALE (Additional Living Expenses), Business Impacts. Each workstream has its own documentation, evidence, and negotiation track.

Technology deployed: 3D LiDAR scanning for structural documentation, AI Vision for inventory from photos, AI Doc Scan for receipts and invoices, Smart Ledger for payment tracking, Xactimate estimates for claim valuation.

Post-Settlement (Life 2.0): After the insurance settles and Loti collects its contingency fee, the relationship doesn't end. Loti provides annual Policy Health Checks (reviewing the homeowner's insurance to ensure proper coverage going forward), seasonal RallyBacks (relevant content for their situation), home-hardening guidance, and risk reduction tips. This ongoing relationship is key for referrals and eventual conversion to The Suite subscription.
❌ CLOSED LOST

The deal didn't close. One of 19 disqualified reasons is recorded in disqualified_reasons, and the closed_lost_reason property captures any additional context. But the journey doesn't necessarily end:

If renurture_eligible = Yes, the contact is re-enrolled in the Lead Nurturing Workflow. This means they'll continue receiving educational content and may re-engage in the future — their situation might change (new incident, insurance issues discovered later, neighbor referral). The system keeps the door open.
