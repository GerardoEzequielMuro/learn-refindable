SEO Improvement Opportunities
Prioritized by potential traffic & local impact · Updated May 2026
🚨
Homepage is barely ranking — only 3.5% CTR with 195K impressions
The homepage gets 195,735 impressions but only 6,879 clicks (3.5% CTR) and ranks avg #20.9. This is a massive lost opportunity. Optimize the homepage title tag and meta description for local intent: target "Internet Service Provider in Wisconsin Rapids, WI".
Critical
🚨
Order/Service page has near-zero conversions from organic
/order-service/ has 20,064 impressions but only 45 clicks (0.2% CTR). This is the most commercially valuable page — optimize title tag, add schema markup, and create local landing pages per city/county (Manawa, Wautoma, Waupaca, Adams, etc.).
Critical
🚨
93% of traffic is branded — nearly zero non-brand organic visibility
Solarus captures only 48 clicks for "internet providers wisconsin rapids" — a non-brand local query with real commercial intent. Building dedicated service-area pages with local keywords could unlock thousands of net-new monthly visitors currently going to Spectrum or Viasat.
Critical
⚠️
Contact page gets 95K impressions, 0.9% CTR — title/meta issue
/contact-solarus/ ranks avg #4.5 with 95,681 impressions but generates only 854 clicks. Users find it but don't click. Rewrite the meta description to include location and call-to-action (e.g. "Call or chat with Solarus internet support in Wisconsin Rapids").
High
⚠️
GBP weekly posts not published consistently
Google rewards active GMB profiles. Publishing 1–2 weekly posts (promotions, service updates, community news) increases visibility in the local 3-pack. GBP already drives 7,362 clicks — this can be doubled with regular activity.
High
⚠️
No service-area pages for surrounding towns
Solarus serves Adams, Waupaca, Portage, and Juneau counties — but there are no dedicated pages targeting "internet provider in [City], WI". These pages would rank for local queries in each city and dramatically increase addressable organic traffic.
High
⚠️
Small Business page barely visible (10 clicks, 9,623 impressions)
/small-business/ ranks avg #10.4 with almost no traffic. Small business internet is a high-value keyword category. Rewrite the page with specific service details, local business testimonials, and schema markup.
High
💡
Review velocity likely below competitors — actively solicit reviews
Local pack rankings are heavily influenced by review count and recency. Solarus has strong customer satisfaction signals (Facebook shows 5-star praise) — setting up a review request workflow (post-install SMS/email) could significantly improve local pack ranking.
Medium
💡
TV page ranks #11 — opportunity to capture cord-cutting searches
/tv/ ranks avg #11.4 with 29,410 impressions but only 637 clicks (2.2% CTR). Queries like "streaming tv wisconsin rapids" or "cable tv alternative central wisconsin" are growing. Optimize with content targeting cord-cutting intent.
Medium
💡
Wikipedia/directory citations need audit
Local SEO trust signals include consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) citations across directories: Yelp, BBB, Yellow Pages, local Chamber of Commerce. An audit of existing citations and cleanup of any inconsistencies will boost local pack ranking.
Medium
💡
GBP Q&A section is unmanaged — competitors can post misinformation
Google lets anyone post questions and answers on your GBP. Proactively seeding 8–12 FAQ answers (pricing, availability, setup process) takes ownership of the narrative and improves both trust and local ranking signals.
Medium
✅
Outage page is high-intent — add local area status and subscribe CTA
"Solarus outage" and "solarus internet down" together generate 312 clicks. These are frustrated customers. A well-designed outage/status page with an email/SMS alert subscription retains customers and reduces churn.
Quick Win
✅
Structured data (LocalBusiness schema) likely missing
Adding LocalBusiness + ISP schema markup to the homepage and contact page enables rich snippets in Google search results and strengthens GBP association. This is a low-effort technical change with measurable impact on CTR.
Quick Win
✅
Webmail login page drives 50K+ clicks — add a cross-sell banner
The /webmail-login/ page is by far the most visited page (50,528 clicks). Adding a subtle "Upgrade your internet speed" or "Refer a friend" banner here could generate upgrade or referral traffic at zero marketing cost.
