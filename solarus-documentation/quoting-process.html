Quoting Process at a Glance

The six steps from deal record to accepted quote

Every HubSpot Quote at Solarus follows this sequence. Each step builds on the last — a quote cannot be sent until it has at least one line item, and a deal should not advance to Negotiation until a quote has been sent and acknowledged.

1
Open the deal record in HubSpot CRM
Navigate to CRM → Deals → B2B Enterprise Pipeline. Click the deal name. All quoting happens from within the deal record — quotes created outside a deal record will not link correctly.
2
Create the quote from the Quotes card
In the right sidebar, find the Quotes card and click + Add. Choose the Solarus quote template. The quote will inherit the deal's company name and associated contact details.
3
Add line items from the product library
Select products from the Solarus product library. Do not use custom line items for standard services — use the library to maintain pricing consistency and product reporting integrity.
4
Edit line items, quantities, and discounts
Adjust quantities and apply any pre-approved discounts at the line item level. Set billing start dates for recurring services if the start date differs from the quote acceptance date.
5
Review the summary and save
Confirm the Subtotal (due at checkout), Future Payments total, and grand Total. Verify these match the deal Amount field. Save the quote before sending.
✓
Send the quote and update the deal stage
Publish the quote and send via HubSpot. The prospect receives a link to view and e-sign. Update the deal stage to "Quote Sent" and create a follow-up task.
01

Creating a Quote from a Deal Record

Step-by-step instructions to initiate a quote in HubSpot

📌
Always start from the deal record. Quotes created outside a deal record — or created first and then associated — frequently have association errors that break the line item sync with the deal Amount field. Start from the deal. Every time.
1
Navigate to CRM → Deals
Use the top navigation bar in HubSpot. Confirm you are in the B2B Enterprise pipeline, not the general Deals view. Find your deal by name or by scrolling the pipeline board.
CRM → Deals → B2B Enterprise Pipeline
2
Click the deal name to open the deal record
This opens the full deal record. Confirm the deal has a Company associated, at least one Contact, and a populated Amount field before proceeding. If any of these are missing, populate them first.
3
Locate the Quotes card in the right sidebar — click + Add
The Quotes card lives in the right sidebar of the deal record. If you don't see it, click the "Customize" link at the bottom of the sidebar to add it. Click + Add to start a new quote.
4
Select the Solarus quote template from the dropdown
In the right panel, click "Select a quote template" and choose the Solarus B2B template. This ensures correct branding, standard terms language, and the proper signature block. Do not use the default HubSpot template.
5
The quote editor opens — confirm prefilled information
HubSpot will prefill the company name, deal name, and primary contact from the deal record. Verify these are correct before adding line items. The quote name will default to the deal name — adjust if needed (e.g., if sending a revised version).

How to Add Line Items from the Product Library
⌄
1
If no line items exist — click "Select from product library"
This button appears in the center of the blank line items module when no items have been added yet. Clicking it opens the product panel on the right.
2
To add more items after the first — click "Add line item" in the module header
In the upper right of the line items module, click "Add line item." In the right panel, select additional product checkboxes and click Add. You can select multiple products at once before clicking Add.
3
Search by product name, description, or SKU
Use the search bar in the product panel to find the right service tier quickly. You can also use Advanced Filters to filter by product category. For tiered pricing products, click "View tiers" before adding to understand the pricing structure.
4
Select checkbox(es) and click Add
The selected products are added to the quote's line items table. They appear with their standard price from the product library. You can then edit quantities and discounts in the expanded view.
✏️
Custom Line Items — When to Use Them
⌄
⚠️
Default to the product library. Custom line items bypass product-level reporting and can create inconsistent pricing. Only use custom line items for genuinely non-standard, one-off items that don't exist in the library.
✓ Appropriate for custom line items
  • ✓One-off custom equipment (non-standard)
  • ✓Special construction / outside plant work
  • ✓Non-recurring project-specific labor
  • ✓Unique contract credits or adjustments
✗ Do NOT use custom line items for
  • ✗Standard fiber or internet tiers
  • ✗Standard VoIP seat pricing
  • ✗Standard installation fees
  • ✗Any product already in the library
Creating a Custom Line Item
1
Click "Add line item" → Select "Create custom line item"
Requires "Create custom line items" permission. If this option is greyed out, contact your HubSpot admin.
2
Fill in the right panel: name, price, quantity, billing frequency
Be specific with the name — it appears on the quote PDF seen by the prospect. Vague line item names (e.g., "Service") undermine the quote's professionalism.
3
Decide: Save to product library, or quote-only
If this item will be used again, save it to the library. If it's truly one-off, use "Save" without adding to library. Click "Save and add another" if you have multiple custom items to add.
📊
Tiered Pricing Line Items
⌄

Some Solarus products may use tiered pricing — where the unit price changes based on quantity. For example, VoIP seat pricing may decrease at higher volume thresholds.

💡
For tiered pricing products, click "View tiers" before adding the line item to understand which pricing tier applies to the prospect's quantity. After adding, click "View tiers" in the expanded line item editor to see the pricing as you adjust quantities — HubSpot updates the price automatically as you cross tier thresholds.
03

Editing Line Items & Applying Discounts

Modifying quantities, prices, discounts, and column configuration

1
Click the line items module → "Edit in expanded view"
The expanded view gives you the full line item editing interface with all columns visible. This is where you adjust quantities, add discounts, and configure billing.
Line Items module → Edit in expanded view (upper right)
2
Edit columns displayed — freeze important columns
Click "Edit columns" in the upper right of the line items table to choose which columns appear (Name, Unit Price, Quantity, Net Price, Unit Discount, Billing Frequency, Tax Rate). Freeze the Name column so it stays visible when scrolling horizontally through wide tables.
3
Apply discounts at the line item level
In the Unit Discount column, click the dropdown to select percentage (%) or currency ($). Enter the discount amount. Note: HubSpot does not support a single discount applied to the entire quote — discounts must be set per line item.
⚠️
Discount limit: Discounts above [your threshold]% require manager approval before the quote is sent. Log the approval as a deal Note before sending. Sending a quote with an unapproved discount that later gets pulled back requires a reissue and harms buyer trust.
⚙️
Line Item Actions: Edit, Clone, Delete
⌄
Edit
Modifies the line item on this quote only. The product library is not affected — standard pricing is preserved for future quotes.
Clone
Creates a duplicate line item with identical details. Useful for quoting the same service to multiple locations (adjust the item name after cloning).
Delete
Removes the line item from this quote. This does not affect the product library or other quotes.
💡
To access these options: hover over a line item row in the expanded view, click Actions, then choose Edit, Clone, or Delete. To reorder line items, use the drag handle on the left side of each row.
04

Billing Start Dates

Configuring when recurring charges begin for each line item

For Solarus B2B deals, recurring service billing (monthly fiber, VoIP, managed WiFi) often doesn't start on the day the quote is signed — it starts when service is provisioned, which may be 30–90 days out. HubSpot allows you to configure this per line item.

On effective date (default)
Billing starts immediately when the quote is accepted / checkout occurs. Use this for one-time fees like installation that are collected at signing.
Custom Date
Billing starts on a specific future date. Use this when you have a firm provisioning date confirmed with the technical team. Select the date in the calendar picker that appears.
Delayed start (days)
Billing starts X days after checkout. Use when you know the lead time but not the exact calendar date. Enter the number of days in the field that appears.
Delayed start (months)
Billing starts X months after checkout. Use for deals where service commencement is measured in months — e.g., large fiber builds where provisioning takes 2–3 months.
⚠️
Stripe requirement for delayed recurring billing: If using HubSpot Payments (Stripe), at least one line item must be due at checkout even if your recurring services are all delayed-start. You cannot have a quote where every line item has a future billing date with zero due today. Typically, the installation or setup fee serves as the at-checkout item.
💡
How it appears to the buyer: Line items with a future billing date will show in the quote summary under "Future payments" — separate from the Subtotal (due at checkout). The grand Total will include all items regardless of billing date. This is visible and clear to the prospect in the quote view.

Quote Summary & Totals

Understanding the three total fields and their relationship to the deal Amount

The Summary section of the quote shows three distinct totals. Understanding the difference between them is important — the deal's Amount field in HubSpot should reflect the Total (all items), not just the Subtotal.

Quote Summary — How Your Buyer Will Be Charged
Subtotal
Items due at checkout only (on effective date)
Items due today
Future Payments
Items with delayed billing start dates
Recurring + deferred items
Total
All items regardless of payment date
All items combined
✓ Before Saving
  • ✓Total matches deal Amount field in HubSpot
  • ✓All recurring items show correct billing frequency
  • ✓Discounts are entered correctly (% or $)
  • ✓Billing start dates confirmed with tech team
  • ✓Quote name is correct (includes company + revision if applicable)
📊 Deal Amount Sync

HubSpot will prompt you to sync the quote's Total to the deal Amount field when you save. Always accept this sync — it ensures your pipeline value accurately reflects what you're actually proposing.

⚠️
If the deal Amount is higher or lower than the quote Total after sync, investigate before sending. The discrepancy often means a line item was changed without updating the quote, or the wrong quantity was entered.
🧾
Tax Rates on Line Items
⌄

Tax rates can be applied at the individual line item level in the expanded view. To see the Tax Rate column, click "Edit columns" and add it to your view.

⚠️
Important limitation: Automated sales tax collection is only available in legacy HubSpot Quotes — not in the current Quotes system used by Solarus. Tax rates must be set manually per line item. Confirm applicable Wisconsin tax rates with your finance team before applying them to quotes.
06

Sending & Tracking Quotes

Publishing the quote, delivering it to the prospect, and monitoring response

📤
Publishing and Sending the Quote
⌄
1
Click Save in the quote editor
Save the quote before publishing. HubSpot will prompt to sync the quote total to the deal Amount — accept the sync. The quote is now in "Draft" status.
2
Review the quote preview — confirm it looks correct
Use the preview mode to see exactly what the prospect will see. Check: company name, contact details, all line items, pricing, and the Solarus template branding. Catch errors here, not after sending.
3
Publish the quote (changes status from Draft to Published)
A published quote generates a unique shareable URL. Only published quotes can be sent or signed. Note: once published, you cannot edit the quote — you must create a new version if changes are needed.
4
Send the quote via HubSpot email or copy the link
Sending through HubSpot enables tracking (view notifications) and keeps the outreach logged on the deal timeline. You can also copy the quote URL and include it in your own email — but use the HubSpot send option when possible.
5
Update deal stage to "Quote Sent" and create a follow-up task
Advance the deal in the pipeline and set a follow-up task for 3–5 business days out. Do not wait for the prospect to respond without a scheduled touchpoint.
👁️
Quote Status States & What They Mean
⌄
Status What It Means Next Action
Draft Quote is saved but not sent. Can still be edited. Review and publish when ready to send.
Published Sent to prospect. Quote URL is live. Cannot be edited. Monitor for opens. Follow up if no view after 2 days.
Awaiting Approval Prospect has viewed quote — pending their decision. Schedule a follow-up call to address questions.
Accepted Prospect has e-signed the quote. Deal is effectively closed. Move deal to Closed Won. Begin provisioning handoff.
Rejected Prospect declined this version. Log reason in deal notes. Move to Negotiation stage. Create revised quote if needed.
🔄
Handling Quote Revisions
⌄

Once a quote is published, it is locked. If the prospect requests changes to pricing, scope, or terms, you must create a new quote version. This is not a limitation — it's intentional, and it protects deal integrity.

1
Create a new quote from the same deal record
Click + Add on the Quotes card to start a new quote. All the deal information prefills again. Name this quote with a revision indicator: "[Company] — [Solution] — Rev2"
2
Mark the original quote as "Draft" (or expired)
To prevent confusion, set the previous quote's expiry date to today — this removes it from the active quote view but preserves the record. Do not delete old quotes; the history matters.
3
Log the revision reason in deal notes
Record what changed, why it changed, and whether a discount approval was required. This note is visible to anyone reviewing the deal record and creates accountability in the revision history.
✅ Complete Quote Checklist — Before Sending
  • Quote created from the deal record
  • Solarus template selected
  • Company and contact info correct
  • All line items from product library (no unauthorized custom items)
  • Quantities correct per technical evaluation
  • Discounts applied correctly (pre-approved if above threshold)
  • Billing start dates set for all recurring items
  • Deal Amount = Quote Total (synced)
  • Quote preview reviewed and approved
  • Deal stage updated to "Quote Sent" after sending
