Stage 1: Prospect Identified

The deal exists. You have a company name and at least one contact. The goal here is to confirm the opportunity is real and qualify basic fit criteria before investing discovery time.

Entry Criteria
  • ✓Company record created in HubSpot
  • ✓At least one contact associated
  • ✓Service address or territory confirmed as serviceable
  • →Estimated deal size entered on deal record
Required HubSpot Actions
  • Create deal record under Company
  • Set Deal Owner to responsible AE
  • Log initial outreach as Activity
  • Set Close Date (estimated)
⚠️
Do not advance to Discovery until the service address is confirmed as within Solarus's service territory. Unserviceable prospects should be moved to Closed Lost with reason "Out of Territory."

Stage 2: Discovery

The goal of discovery is to understand the prospect's current infrastructure, bandwidth needs, number of locations, and decision timeline. This stage produces the information required to build an accurate quote.

Key Questions to Log
  • →Current provider and contract expiry
  • →Number of employees / locations
  • →Bandwidth requirements (symmetric vs. asymmetric)
  • →Any dedicated fiber, VoIP, or managed service needs
  • →Budget range (if willing to share)
  • →Decision-making committee (economic buyer, IT lead, etc.)
HubSpot Record Updates
  • Log discovery call notes under Activity
  • Add all stakeholders as Contacts, associate to Company
  • Update deal Amount with refined estimate
  • Set Contact roles (see Multi-Stakeholder section)
✅
Exit to Technical Eval when: you have confirmed the service solution category (Fiber Internet, Dedicated Ethernet, Managed WiFi, VoIP) and have all stakeholders identified. The Technical Eval stage will validate what's physically deliverable.

Stage 3: Technical Evaluation

Solarus's technical team confirms serviceability at the specific address, available bandwidth tiers, and any build or installation requirements. This stage gates the quote — you cannot price accurately without a technical sign-off.

📋
Pre-Quote Gate: Technical Eval is a hard dependency for quoting. The products and pricing added to the HubSpot Quote in Stage 4 must reflect the deliverable tiers confirmed in this stage. Quoting before technical sign-off risks misrepresenting what Solarus can deliver.
  • Technical team logs site assessment results in deal Notes
  • Deliverable bandwidth tiers documented on deal record
  • Installation timeline estimated and recorded
  • Any special build requirements flagged

Stage 4: Quote Sent HubSpot Quotes

This is the formal quoting stage. A HubSpot-native Quote is created directly from the deal record, using products from the Solarus product library. The quote must be sent via HubSpot to enable tracking and e-signature.

📄
This stage triggers the Quoting Process
Creating and sending a HubSpot Quote is the primary action in this stage. The Quote links directly to this deal record, syncs line items to the deal Amount, and provides a tracked, signable document for the prospect.
Open Quoting Process Documentation →
Required Before Sending
  • Technical Eval stage completed
  • All line items from product library
  • Quote reviewed internally (pricing approved)
  • Correct contacts associated to deal
After Quote Is Sent
  • Deal stage updated to "Quote Sent"
  • Follow-up task created (3-5 business days)
  • Quote open/view notifications monitored

Stage 5: Negotiation

The prospect has received and reviewed the quote. Negotiation may involve pricing adjustments, contract length changes, or scope modifications. Any changes to pricing or scope must be reflected in a revised HubSpot Quote — do not negotiate verbally without updating the quote record.

⚠️
Quote Revision Protocol: If pricing or line items change during negotiation, create a new quote version rather than editing the original. This preserves a clear pricing history on the deal record. Mark superseded quotes as "Draft" to remove them from the active quote view.
  • Log all negotiation touchpoints as Activities
  • Revise quote if pricing/scope changes (new version)
  • Update deal Amount to match current quote total
  • Flag any approvals needed (discounts over threshold)

Stage 6: Closed Won

The prospect has accepted and signed the quote. The deal is marked as Closed Won. This triggers downstream processes including provisioning handoff and account creation.

🎉
Closed Won criteria: The HubSpot Quote is in "Accepted" status (prospect has e-signed), or written confirmation of acceptance has been logged as an Activity on the deal.
Deal Closure Checklist
  • ✓Quote in Accepted status
  • Deal Amount matches signed quote
  • Close Date updated to actual date
  • Closed Won reason logged
Post-Win Actions
  • Provisioning ticket created externally
  • Contact roles updated (now Customer)
  • Company record flagged as active customer
  • Deal handed to account management

Account & Deal Setup

Required fields and record structure for B2B enterprise deals

Every Solarus enterprise opportunity exists as a three-level structure in HubSpot: Company → Contact(s) → Deal. These records must be properly associated before a Quote can be created. Misassociation is the most common cause of quote errors.

🏢
Company Record — Required Fields
⌄
Field Purpose Required?
Company Name Displays on all quotes and deal records Required
Service Address Used to confirm serviceability before quoting Required
Industry Segmentation and reporting Optional
Number of Employees Helps size bandwidth and solution requirements Required
Current Provider Competitive intelligence, contract timing Optional
📋
Deal Record — Required Fields
⌄
Field Purpose Required?
Deal Name Format: [Company] — [Service Type] — [Year] Required
Deal Owner The AE responsible for this opportunity Required
Deal Stage Current pipeline position Required
Amount Expected ARR — syncs with Quote totals Required
Close Date Estimated contract date — used in pipeline forecasting Required
Pipeline Must be set to "B2B Enterprise" pipeline Required
Solution Type Fiber, Dedicated Ethernet, Managed WiFi, VoIP, Bundle Required
💡
Use the naming convention [Company] — [Solution] — [Year] for deal names. Example: Riverwood Clinic — Fiber 1G — 2025. This makes pipeline views scannable and quote PDFs clearly labeled.
👤
Creating a Deal from a Deal Record
⌄
1
Navigate to CRM → Deals
Use the top navigation bar. Ensure you are viewing the B2B Enterprise pipeline, not the residential pipeline.
CRM → Deals → B2B Enterprise Pipeline
2
Click "Create deal" in the top right
Fill all required fields listed above. Associate the deal to both a Company and at least one Contact before saving.
3
Associate the Company and Contacts
In the "Associations" section of the deal creation panel, search for the existing Company record. If it doesn't exist, create the Company first. Do the same for the primary Contact.
4
Set Deal Stage to "Prospect Identified" and save
The deal is now visible in your pipeline. Log your first activity (call, email, or note) immediately to establish a timeline.

Multi-Stakeholder Management

Mapping enterprise buying committees within HubSpot deal records

Enterprise deals at Solarus typically involve 2–5 stakeholders across IT, finance, and operations. HubSpot's deal-to-contact associations allow you to track every stakeholder with their specific role. Failing to map the full buying committee is a leading cause of late-stage deal stalls.

Economic Buyer
Has budget authority and final sign-off
Often the CFO, VP of Operations, or Business Owner. The quote must be addressed to or include this person. They sign the agreement.
Quote recipient
Technical Evaluator
Validates technical requirements and solution fit
Typically the IT Director or Network Manager. Involved heavily during Technical Eval stage. Their sign-off gates the quote.
Stage 3 key contact
Champion / Internal Advocate
Wants the project to succeed, influences internally
Often your primary day-to-day contact. They move the deal internally but may not have final authority. Equip them to sell upward.
Primary AE contact
Blocker
Can halt or delay the deal
Procurement, legal, or a stakeholder whose needs haven't been addressed. Identify and log blockers early. Document their objections in deal notes.
Log objections
💡
HubSpot contact roles: HubSpot Sales Hub Enterprise supports custom contact-to-deal association labels. Use these to tag each contact with their buyer role directly on the deal record. This makes it immediately visible in deal views who your champion is, who the signer is, and where objections are coming from.

The Quoting Trigger Point

When, why, and how quoting fits into the sales process

📄
Quoting begins at Stage 4: "Quote Sent"
A HubSpot Quote is created from the deal record at the point when technical evaluation is complete and pricing is ready to present formally. The quote acts as both the formal proposal document and the agreement mechanism — when the prospect accepts the quote in HubSpot, it serves as electronic acceptance of the deal terms.
Full Quoting Process Documentation →
✅
Pre-Quoting Checklist (Complete Before Creating Quote)
⌄
  • Technical evaluation complete. The deliverable bandwidth tier, installation timeline, and any build costs are confirmed by Solarus's technical team.
  • Deal record fully populated. Company, all contacts, deal stage, deal amount, solution type, and close date are all filled in.
  • Economic buyer identified and associated. The person who will sign must be associated to the deal as a Contact. Their name and email will appear on the quote.
  • Pricing confirmed internally. Any discounts beyond standard thresholds have been pre-approved before the quote is built.
  • Products confirmed in the product library. The exact service tiers being quoted exist in HubSpot's product library with accurate pricing.
⚠️
Common Quoting Mistakes to Avoid
⌄
!
Quoting before Technical Eval is complete
Results in incorrect bandwidth tier or pricing on the quote. Requires a reissue, which undermines credibility with the prospect. Always wait for technical sign-off.
!
Editing the original quote instead of creating a new version
Destroys the pricing history on the deal. If a prospect questions what changed, you have no record. Always create a new quote for revisions.
!
Using custom line items for standard products
Custom line items don't link to the product library and break pricing consistency. Only use custom line items for genuinely non-standard items (one-off installation costs, custom equipment).

Deal Closure

Handling Closed Won and Closed Lost with data integrity

🎉 Closed Won
  • ✓Quote status = "Accepted" in HubSpot
  • Deal Amount = final signed quote value
  • Close Date = actual signed date
  • Deal stage moved to "Closed Won"
  • Closed Won reason selected from picklist
  • Company Type updated to "Customer"
📌
Log a deal note summarizing the win — solution sold, key stakeholders, and any relevant implementation details. This is referenced during provisioning handoff.
✗ Closed Lost
  • Deal stage moved to "Closed Lost"
  • Closed Lost reason selected (required)
  • Competitor (if applicable) logged
  • Notes on why the deal was lost
  • Future re-engage date set if relevant
⚠️
Closed Lost reasons are used in pipeline reporting. Do not select "Other" — always choose the most accurate reason from the list. Accurate lost reasons drive product and pricing decisions.
📊
Reporting note: HubSpot's deal reporting relies on close dates, amounts, and stage transition history. A deal that skips stages or has inaccurate close dates will distort pipeline velocity and forecasting reports. Always keep records current as you work through each stage.
