I

Sales Process

How to work leads and opportunities in HubSpot

End-to-end walkthrough — from the moment a lead is assigned to you through converting it into a deal and creating a quote.

1Receive the lead notification
When a new lead is assigned to you, you will receive a HubSpot email notification. Open the email and click View in HubSpot to go directly to the record.
2Lead starts in the "New" stage
In HubSpot, the lead will usually start in the New stage. This means the opportunity has been assigned, but no outreach has been made yet.
3Review the lead record before taking action
You will be able to see:
  • The lead stage
  • The lead owner
  • The contact linked to the lead
  • Any previous activity, such as page views, form submissions, or other engagement history
4Use the three main actions from the lead record
From the lead record, there are three main actions you will use:
  • Make a phone call
  • Create an email
  • Create a note
5Call the lead via 3CX
To call the lead, click Make phone call and use the Call via 3CX option. This will open 3CX so you can place the call.
6Email the lead from HubSpot
To email the lead, click Create an email. HubSpot will open the email composer with your signature already included, so you can write and send the message directly from the record.
7Create internal notes
To leave internal context, create a note. This is useful for adding details about conversations, next steps, or anything relevant to the opportunity.
8Turn notes into follow-up tasks
If needed, turn the note into a follow-up task. For example, if the contact asks you to call back in two business days, you can write that in the note and also create a task at the same time so the follow-up is not missed.
9Move the lead through stages
As you continue outreach, move the lead through the correct lead stages. For example, if you have already tried reaching out more than once, the stage may move from New to Second Attempt.
10Convert the lead into a deal
Once the opportunity becomes more qualified and there is real interest in moving forward, convert the lead into a deal. This is how the process moves from an early lead into an active sales opportunity.
11Complete the deal fields
When creating the deal, complete the key fields:
Deal name
Pipeline Sales Pipeline
Deal stage New Opportunity
Deal owner
Deal type
Expected close date if known
Amount if known
Some fields can be left blank if the information is not available yet.
12Access the deal record
After the deal is created, you can access it from:
  • The lead record
  • The Deals area in HubSpot
  • CRM → Deals
13Manage the deal
In the deal record, you can continue managing the opportunity by:
  • Logging notes
  • Logging calls
  • Sending emails
  • Reviewing related activity
  • Creating a quote when the opportunity is ready
14Create a quote
When it is time to send pricing, go to the right sidebar in the deal record and click Add under Quotes. This is where the quote creation process begins.
Important Notes
Leads are for earlier-stage opportunities that still need outreach and qualification. Deals are for warmer opportunities where the conversation is moving forward and quoting may be needed. Always keep notes and follow-up tasks updated so nothing gets missed.
II

Quoting Process

How to create a quote in HubSpot

Step-by-step guide to building, reviewing, and sending quotes from the deal record.

1Open the deal and add a quote
Open the deal you want to quote. Quotes are created from deals once the opportunity is ready to move forward. In the right sidebar, go to Quotes and click Add.
2Confirm contact and company details
HubSpot will open the quote builder. Confirm the contact included in the quote. If there is an associated company, it may also appear here. Click Next. Your user details and Solarus company details should already be filled in. Click Next again.
3Add products from the library
In Review line items, add the products or services for the quote:
  • Click Select from product library
  • Search and choose the products needed
  • Click Add
4Review and adjust line items
Review the line items and adjust anything needed:
  • Quantity
  • Unit discount in % or $
  • Subscription term
  • Delete any item added by mistake if needed
5Review subtotal and add extras
Review the subtotal. If needed, add:
  • A one-time discount
  • A one-time fee
  • A one-time tax
6Skip Payment schedule for now
Ignore Payment schedule for now. The video covers it in case you need it in the near future. It is not part of the current process, so leave it off and click Next.
7Set up e-signature
In the signature step:
  • Select HubSpot e-signature
  • Make the customer contact the required signer
  • Do not add a countersigner unless specifically needed
  • Keep payment collection off
Note
The required signer does not need a verification method, so another person can sign on their behalf if needed.
8Select the correct quote template
In the quote settings, make sure the template selected is: B2B | Solarus Default New Edits, 03.26
9Add quote name, expiration and terms
Add the quote name and update the quote URL slug only if needed. Then choose the expiration date for the quote. If needed, add extra purchase terms using Insert Snippet. Only add terms that apply to the products or services being quoted.
Warning
Do not add terms that are already built into the template, such as the standard acceptance, termination, tariff notice, or shipping disclaimer.
10Review the final preview
Review the final preview carefully:
  • Products and services
  • Subtotal and discounts
  • Purchase terms
  • Signature section
When everything looks right, click Create.
11Go to quote and send it
After the quote is created, click Go to quote. From there, you can:
  • Click Write email with quote
  • Review the final quote URL in a separate tab if needed before sending
12Compose and send the email
In the email box:
  • Update the subject line if needed
  • Write your message
  • Insert the quote link
  • Send it to the customer for signature
Important Notes
Always create quotes from the deal record, not separately. The product library is where you should pull the services and fees from. The customer will receive the quote link, open it, sign it, and submit it from their side.

Sales Workspace & Tasks

How to use the Sales Workspace in HubSpot

Your daily hub for managing leads, deals, tasks, and activity — all in one place.

1Go to Sales > Sales Workspace
A good best practice is to bookmark this page so you can access it quickly every day.
2Use the Summary section as your main starting point
This is where you can manage your leads, deals, tasks, calendar, and sales activity in one place.
3Review your Tasks first
Here you can see:
  • All pending tasks
  • Task types like to-dos and calls
  • Quick filters such as Due today, Overdue, and Due tomorrow
4Click Start tasks to work through them one by one
HubSpot will take you directly to the related record so you can complete the action from there.
5For each task, choose the right action
  • Complete it if the action is done
  • Add a note if you need to leave context
  • Reschedule it if it needs to be pushed to a later date
6Call directly from the task
If the task is a call and a phone number is available, you can call directly from the record, add notes, and then move on to the next task.
7Check Suggested tasks and meeting follow-ups
HubSpot may show recommended next steps based on your recent activity, such as following up after a meeting.
8Review stalled deals
These are deals with no activity in the last 14 days, so they are useful to review and reactivate if needed.
9Use the Schedule section
View your calendar and tasks together. You can switch between calendar and list views to manage your day more easily.
10Check your Insights
Get a quick view of your activity and pipeline:
  • Calls made
  • Emails sent
  • Email replies
  • Lead status
  • Pipeline activity for the month or quarter
11Use the Activity Feed to track contact engagement
This shows actions such as:
  • Email opens
  • Link clicks
  • Booked meetings
  • Form activity
12Review your Leads
In the Leads section, switch to My leads to focus only on your own records. Here you can review lead stages and the most recent activity on each lead.
13Review your Deals
In the Deals section, switch to My deals to review your active opportunities. You can see:
  • Which stage each deal is in
  • How many deals you have in each stage
  • The latest activity on each deal
14Use saved views in Tasks
In the Tasks area, you can use saved views to organize work by type, such as quote-related tasks only.
15Use the Dashboard to track your performance
This may include:
  • Tasks past due
  • Inactive prospects
  • Deals by stage
  • Deal progression
  • Quotes sent
Best Practices
Start your day in Sales Workspace. Review tasks first. Use My leads and My deals to keep your view focused. Update tasks, notes, and records as you go. Check the activity feed and dashboard regularly to stay on top of follow-ups and pipeline health.
IV

3CX Calling

Install the 3CX extension on your browser

Chrome

1Open Chrome
Go to the 3CX Click2Call extension page in the Chrome Web Store.
2Add to Chrome
Click Add to Chrome. A permissions pop-up will appear. Review it, then click Add extension.
3Confirm installation
Wait for Chrome to finish installing. Click the Extensions icon to the right of the address bar and look for 3CX Click2Call. If needed, pin it so it stays visible in the toolbar.

Edge

Best option: install it from the Microsoft Edge Add-ons store.

1Open Edge and find the extension
Go to the 3CX Click2Call page in Microsoft Edge Add-ons.
2Install the extension
Click Get. Review the permissions prompt, then confirm the install. Wait for Edge to finish installing it.
3Confirm installation
Click the Extensions icon to the right of the address bar and check for 3CX Click2Call.
Alternative for Edge
You can also install the Chrome version in Edge. Open the Chrome Web Store page for 3CX Click2Call. If Edge shows a banner at the top, click Allow extensions from other stores, then click Add to Chrome and confirm.

How to call from HubSpot using 3CX

Make sure the 3CX browser extension is installed and active before starting.

1Option 1: Call from a lead or contact record
  • Open the lead or contact record
  • Click Call or Make a phone call
  • Select Call phone number via 3CX
  • The 3CX window will open automatically with the phone number ready
  • Click the call button in 3CX to place the call
2Option 2: Call from a deal
  • Open the deal record
  • Click the associated contact
  • In the contact record, find the phone number you want to call
  • Click the phone number and select Call phone number via 3CX
  • The 3CX window will open with the number ready to dial
  • Click the call button in 3CX to start the call
Important
Do not use HubSpot's default calling option if the goal is to call through 3CX. Always click the phone number or the option that says Call phone number via 3CX.
Extra Tip
In the 3CX window, you can also search for HubSpot contacts by name. If the contact is synced, 3CX will show it as a CRM result, so you can start the call directly from there.
